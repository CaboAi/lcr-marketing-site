Moving to Los Cabos: The Ultimate Guide to Finding Long-Term Rentals

Discover how to navigate the luxury rental market in Baja California Sur, from hidden beach gems to exclusive gated communities.

Caleb Vance Local Expert & Relocation Consultant Oct 24, 2023 · 12 min read · Updated 2 days ago

Los Cabos has long been known as a premier vacation destination, but in recent years, it has transformed into a global hub for remote workers and expats seeking a high-quality lifestyle. Transitioning from a week-long visitor to a long-term resident requires a different strategic approach to finding a home.

Understanding the Neighborhoods

The first step in your relocation journey is deciding between the vibrant energy of Cabo San Lucas and the colonial charm of San Jose del Cabo. Each offer distinct rental markets:

  • Cabo San Lucas: High demand, proximity to nightlife, and iconic views of Land's End.
  • San Jose del Cabo: More tranquil, art-centric, and preferred by many long-term expat families.
  • The Corridor: Luxury gated communities situated between the two towns, offering privacy and beach access.

Rental Agreements and Legalities

Unlike short-term stays, long-term rentals in Mexico (typically 6 months to a year) often require a formal contract. It is highly recommended to have these reviewed by a local relocation expert. Most landlords will ask for a one-month security deposit and the first month's rent upfront.

What to Look for in a Property

Consider the utility costs. Electricity (CFE) can be significant during the summer months due to air conditioning. Ensure your rental agreement specifies who is responsible for these payments and check if the home has solar panels—a major plus in the Cabo sun.
