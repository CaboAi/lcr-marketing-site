Safety Tips for Renters

Follow these guidelines to protect yourself and ensure a smooth rental experience in Los Cabos.

location_on

Visit the Property in Person

Always schedule an in-person visit before signing any rental agreement. Verify that the property matches the listing photos and description. If you are unable to visit, ask a trusted local contact or hire a relocation service to inspect on your behalf.

block

Never Send Money Outside the Platform

All payments should be processed through Los Cabos Rental App. Never wire money, send cash, or use informal payment methods requested by a landlord outside the platform. If someone asks you to pay outside the system, report them immediately.

badge

Verify the Landlord's Identity

Check that the landlord has a verified badge on their profile. Our verification system confirms their identity and property ownership. Be cautious of landlords who have not completed verification or who pressure you to make quick decisions.

description

Read Your Lease Agreement Carefully

Review every clause of the rental contract before signing. Pay attention to the lease duration, payment terms, deposit conditions, maintenance responsibilities, and early termination clauses. If the lease is in Spanish, request a certified translation or use our bilingual contract templates.

photo_camera

Document the Property at Move-In

Take detailed photographs and videos of every room, including any existing damage, on the day you move in. Use our in-app move-in checklist to record the condition of walls, floors, appliances, fixtures, and furniture. This protects your security deposit when you move out.

flag

Report Suspicious Listings Immediately

If a listing seems too good to be true, it probably is. Watch for warning signs: unusually low prices, landlords who refuse video calls, pressure to pay immediately, or listings with stock photography. Use the "Report" button on any listing or contact our safety team directly.

Safety Tips for Landlords

Protect your property and ensure reliable tenancy with these best practices.

person_search

Screen Tenants Thoroughly

Use our built-in tenant screening tools to review rental applications, verify employment and income, and check references from previous landlords. A thorough screening process dramatically reduces the risk of payment issues or property damage.

gavel

Use a Legally Binding Contract

Always use a formal rental contract that complies with Mexican civil law. Our platform provides bilingual contract templates reviewed by local attorneys. Never rely on verbal agreements -- a written contract protects both you and your tenant in case of disputes.

inventory

Create a Detailed Property Inventory

Before the tenant moves in, document every item and the condition of the property with photos and a written inventory list. Have both parties sign the inventory. This serves as the baseline for security deposit deductions and prevents disputes at move-out.

security

Maintain Adequate Insurance

Ensure your property is covered by appropriate insurance that includes rental liability. Consider requiring tenants to obtain renter's insurance as a lease condition. Proper coverage protects you from unexpected damages, natural disasters, and liability claims.
